Biography: The Journey of Emraan Hashmi

Emraan Hashmi, born on March 24, 1979, is an acclaimed Indian actor who primarily works in Hindi cinema. Known for his unconventional film choices and intense performances, he has established himself as a prominent figure in the industry. Hashmi's career began as an assistant director for the 2002 horror film 'Raaz' before making his acting debut with the 2003 crime thriller 'Footpath'.

His breakthrough came with the 2004 erotic thriller 'Murder', which was a major commercial success. This was followed by a series of successful films, often featuring him in complex and morally ambiguous roles, earning him the moniker "Serial Kisser" early in his career. However, he soon diversified his roles, proving his acting prowess in various genres.

Early Life and Background

Emraan Hashmi was born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, to Anwar Hashmi and Mahira Hashmi. His father was a businessman, and his mother was a homemaker. He is the nephew of filmmaker Mahesh Bhatt and producer Mukesh Bhatt, and the cousin of actresses Pooja Bhatt and Alia Bhatt, and director Mohit Suri. He completed his schooling at Jamnabai Narsee School and pursued a Bachelor of Commerce from Sydenham College, Mumbai.

Career Highlights and Evolution

Hashmi's career trajectory is marked by a steady evolution from a romantic thriller hero to a versatile actor capable of portraying diverse characters. Some of his critically acclaimed and commercially successful films include 'Gangster' (2006), 'Jannat' (2008), 'Raaz 2' (2009), 'Once Upon a Time in Mumbaai' (2010), 'The Dirty Picture' (2011), 'Shanghai' (2012), and 'Ek Thi Daayan' (2013).

He has often collaborated with directors like Mohit Suri and Mahesh Bhatt, delivering memorable performances in films known for their strong narratives and popular music. In recent years, he has continued to challenge himself with roles in films like 'Baadshaho' (2017), 'Why Cheat India' (2019), and 'Mumbai Saga' (2021), and 'Tiger 3' (2023) where he played the antagonist, showcasing his range.

Personal Life

Emraan Hashmi is married to Parveen Shahani, and they have a son named Ayaan Hashmi. He is known for being a private person and maintaining a low profile outside of his professional commitments. He is also an advocate for cancer awareness, having shared his son's battle with the disease.
